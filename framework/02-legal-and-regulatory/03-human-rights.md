## Purpose

LLM deployments in the public sector must be assessed against the Human Rights Act 1998 and the European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR), which remain part of UK law.

## Relevant Rights

| Article | Right | LLM Relevance |
|---------|-------|---------------|
| Article 6 | Right to a fair trial | LLMs must not make or influence judicial/quasi-judicial decisions without safeguards |
| Article 8 | Right to respect for private and family life | LLM processing of personal data, surveillance implications |
| Article 10 | Freedom of expression | LLM content filtering must not unduly restrict expression |
| Article 14 | Prohibition of discrimination | LLM outputs must not discriminate on any ground |

## Requirements

- [ ] Human rights impact screening completed
- [ ] Article 8 implications assessed (privacy, data processing)
- [ ] Article 14 implications assessed (discrimination in outputs)
- [ ] If LLM is used in justice/enforcement contexts, Article 6 implications assessed
- [ ] Proportionality of LLM use assessed against rights interference
- [ ] Legal advisor sign-off obtained (per ETA Point 6)

## Output

Human rights screening record documenting relevant articles, risk assessment, and mitigations.
