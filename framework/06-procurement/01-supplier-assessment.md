## Purpose

Most UK public sector LLM deployments involve procuring from third-party providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Microsoft, etc.). The AI Procurement Guidelines provide specific criteria for evaluating AI suppliers.

## Supplier Evaluation Criteria (per AI Procurement Guidelines)

| Criteria | What to Assess |
|----------|---------------|
| AI ethics framework | Does the supplier have a documented AI ethics framework? How is it applied? |
| Accountability mechanisms | Who is accountable for algorithmic outputs? What happens when things go wrong? |
| Bias prevention | What bias testing and mitigation strategies does the supplier employ? |
| Reproducibility | Can results be reproduced and tested independently? |
| Team diversity | Does the supplier's team include diverse perspectives? |
| Explainability | Can the supplier explain how the model produces outputs? |
| IP and data ownership | Who owns the data, the model, and the outputs? |
| Knowledge transfer | Will the supplier transfer knowledge so the organisation is not dependent? |
| Training data provenance | Can the supplier document where training data came from? |
| Security practices | What security certifications and practices does the supplier maintain? |

## Requirements

### Pre-procurement

- [ ] Commercial colleagues engaged early (AIP Principle 8)
- [ ] Market engagement conducted (accessible to SMEs and startups, per PROC Consideration 5)
- [ ] Problem-focused requirements written (not prescribing specific models, per PROC Consideration 6)
- [ ] Evaluation criteria defined before going to market

### Tender evaluation

- [ ] Multidisciplinary evaluation panel (not just technical, per PROC Consideration 2)
- [ ] Ethical evaluation weighted alongside technical evaluation
- [ ] Supplier's bias mitigation strategies assessed
- [ ] Supplier's transparency and explainability capabilities assessed
- [ ] References and case studies reviewed

### Due diligence

- [ ] Supplier's data handling practices verified
- [ ] Security certifications confirmed
- [ ] Business continuity and disaster recovery assessed
- [ ] Financial stability assessed (risk of supplier disappearing)
- [ ] Vendor lock-in risk assessed

## Output

1. Supplier evaluation scorecard
2. Due diligence report
3. Recommendation with justification
