## Purpose

The AI Regulation White Paper requires "appropriate transparency and explainability" — people should be able to understand AI outputs and decisions. The AI Procurement Guidelines explicitly state: prioritise explainable AI over "black-box" solutions. The AI Playbook acknowledges this is inherently challenging for LLMs.

## The Explainability Challenge for LLMs

LLMs are fundamentally opaque. The AI Playbook explicitly identifies "explainability challenges — neural networks operate as black boxes" as a core limitation. This means organisations must find practical ways to provide meaningful explanations despite this limitation.

## Levels of Explanation (per Turing Institute)

| Audience | What they need | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| Citizens / end users | Plain-language explanation of how AI affects their case | "This response was generated by AI. A human advisor has reviewed it." |
| Caseworkers / operators | Understanding of confidence levels and when to override | Confidence scores, flagging uncertain responses |
| Managers / SROs | Aggregate performance and risk information | Dashboards, audit reports |
| Auditors / regulators | Technical documentation and audit trails | Model cards, decision logs, ATRS records |

## Requirements

### User-facing explainability (SS Point 4)

- [ ] Users are told when AI is generating or influencing content
- [ ] The AI's role in any decision is explained in plain language
- [ ] Users understand what the AI can and cannot do
- [ ] Error recovery is straightforward when AI outputs are wrong
- [ ] Explanations are accessible (clear language, appropriate format)

### Operator-level explainability

- [ ] Confidence indicators available for LLM outputs
- [ ] Operators know when to trust vs. override the LLM
- [ ] Decision audit trails are maintained
- [ ] Operators are trained on LLM limitations

### System-level explainability

- [ ] Model card or equivalent documentation maintained
- [ ] Training data provenance documented
- [ ] Known limitations and failure modes documented
- [ ] ATRS record provides public-facing system description

### Procurement requirement (PROC Consideration 8)

- [ ] Supplier provides sufficient transparency into model behaviour
- [ ] No unacceptable "black box" dependency
- [ ] Vendor lock-in risk assessed against explainability needs
- [ ] Contractual right to model documentation
