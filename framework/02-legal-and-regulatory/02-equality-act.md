## Purpose

The Equality Act 2010 and the Public Sector Equality Duty (PSED) are legally binding. Any LLM deployed in the public sector must not discriminate against individuals based on protected characteristics.

## Protected Characteristics

LLM outputs must be assessed for bias across all nine protected characteristics:

1. Age
2. Disability
3. Gender reassignment
4. Marriage and civil partnership
5. Pregnancy and maternity
6. Race
7. Religion or belief
8. Sex
9. Sexual orientation

## Requirements

### Equality Impact Assessment (EIA)

- [ ] EIA conducted before LLM deployment
- [ ] Assessment covers direct discrimination (treating someone less favourably)
- [ ] Assessment covers indirect discrimination (neutral policy with disproportionate impact)
- [ ] Assessment covers all nine protected characteristics
- [ ] Mitigations documented for any identified risks
- [ ] EIA reviewed and signed off by appropriate authority

### Public Sector Equality Duty (Section 149)

Public bodies must have due regard to:
- [ ] Eliminating discrimination, harassment, and victimisation
- [ ] Advancing equality of opportunity between those who share a protected characteristic and those who do not
- [ ] Fostering good relations between those who share a protected characteristic and those who do not

### Accessibility (SS Point 5, TCoP Point 2)

- [ ] LLM-powered interfaces meet WCAG 2.2 AA standard
- [ ] Service tested with assistive technologies (screen readers, voice control, etc.)
- [ ] Non-AI alternative paths exist for users who cannot use the AI component
- [ ] Content generated by the LLM is accessible (clear language, appropriate structure)

### ETA Framework requirements

- [ ] Multidisciplinary, diverse team assembled to counter bias (ETA Point 2)
- [ ] Bias and safety bounties considered to find discriminatory elements (ETA Point 2)
- [ ] Diverse, quality datasets used (ETA Point 2)
