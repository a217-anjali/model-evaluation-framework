## Purpose

The ATRS is **mandatory** for all government departments and arm's-length bodies delivering public or frontline services since December 2024. It is recommended (non-mandatory) for the broader public sector. Any LLM deployed in these organisations must have a completed and published ATRS record.

## ATRS Requirements

### What must be documented

- [ ] **Tool name and description** — What the LLM does in plain language
- [ ] **Purpose** — Why it is being used and what problem it solves
- [ ] **How it works** — Description of the algorithmic approach (accessible to non-technical readers)
- [ ] **Data used** — What data the LLM processes and where it comes from
- [ ] **Impact on decisions** — How the LLM influences or contributes to decisions
- [ ] **Human oversight** — What human review is in place
- [ ] **Performance metrics** — How the system's quality is measured
- [ ] **Limitations** — Known weaknesses and failure modes
- [ ] **Risk mitigations** — What safeguards are in place

### Publication requirements

- [ ] Record completed using the ATRS template
- [ ] Record published in the ATRS public repository
- [ ] Information is complete, open, understandable, easily-accessible, and free
- [ ] Record kept up to date as the system changes

### Contractual obligations (per AI Playbook Principle 8)

- [ ] If using a third-party LLM, ATRS information categories are referenced in the contract
- [ ] Supplier provides necessary information to complete the ATRS record
- [ ] Transparency requirements are included in procurement specifications
