## Purpose

Apply the government's Data and AI Ethics Framework (7 principles, updated December 2025) to the LLM deployment. This section also integrates the Turing Institute's complementary frameworks.

## The 7 Principles Applied to LLMs

### 1. Transparency
- [ ] It is clear to users when they are interacting with an LLM
- [ ] The purpose and limitations of the LLM are communicated in accessible language
- [ ] Information about how the LLM contributes to decisions is published
- [ ] ATRS record completed (see Section 05)

### 2. Accountability
- [ ] Senior Responsible Owner (SRO) named
- [ ] Governance structure documented with clear roles
- [ ] Audit trails maintained for LLM-informed decisions
- [ ] Feedback and complaints mechanisms in place
- [ ] The public body accepts responsibility even when using a third-party LLM

### 3. Fairness
- [ ] LLM outputs tested across protected characteristics
- [ ] Fairness metrics applied: demographic parity, equalized odds
- [ ] Training data assessed for representational, societal, aggregation, and measurement bias
- [ ] Accessibility barriers identified and addressed
- [ ] Equality Impact Assessment completed

### 4. Privacy
- [ ] UK GDPR compliance confirmed (see Section 02)
- [ ] Data minimisation applied to prompts and outputs
- [ ] Personal data processing has a documented lawful basis
- [ ] DPIA completed where required

### 5. Safety
- [ ] LLM operates reliably without causing harm
- [ ] Security threats assessed (prompt injection, data leakage, poisoning)
- [ ] Content filtering and validation implemented
- [ ] Fallback mechanisms in place

### 6. Societal Impact
- [ ] Wider social implications assessed (e.g., misinformation, displacement of human roles)
- [ ] Public benefit clearly articulated
- [ ] Impact on vulnerable groups specifically considered

### 7. Environmental Sustainability
- [ ] Compute and energy costs estimated
- [ ] Carbon footprint of inference at scale assessed
- [ ] Sustainable model choices considered (smaller models where sufficient)

## Turing Institute SAFE-D Alignment

| SAFE-D | DEF Equivalent | Additional Considerations |
|--------|---------------|--------------------------|
| Sustainability | Principle 7 (Environmental) + Principle 5 (Safety) | Long-term viability, not just environmental |
| Accountability | Principle 2 | Process-based governance (PBG) framework |
| Fairness | Principle 3 | Four types: data, design, outcome, implementation |
| Explainability | Principle 1 (Transparency) | Different explanations for different audiences |
| Data Stewardship | Principle 4 (Privacy) | Broader than privacy — covers all data governance |
