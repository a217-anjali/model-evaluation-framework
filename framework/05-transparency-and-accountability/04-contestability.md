# 04 — Contestability and Redress

## Sources

| Source | Reference |
|--------|-----------|
| AI Regulation White Paper | Principle 5: Contestability and redress |
| ETA Framework | Point 5: Help users understand how systems impact them |
| ICO AI Guidance | Individual rights (right to contest automated decisions) |
| Service Standard | Point 3: Provide a joined up experience across all channels |

## Purpose

Citizens must be able to challenge LLM-informed decisions and access remedies. The AI Regulation White Paper makes this one of five core regulatory principles. The ETA Framework requires accessible feedback loops and accountable officers for real-time citizen queries.

## Requirements

### Challenge mechanisms

- [ ] Citizens are informed that AI contributed to a decision about them
- [ ] A clear process exists to challenge the decision
- [ ] Challenge route is accessible (not buried in terms and conditions)
- [ ] Challenges are handled by a competent human (not the same LLM)
- [ ] Response timescales are defined and communicated

### Redress

- [ ] If the LLM-informed decision was wrong, a correction process exists
- [ ] Affected citizens receive an appropriate remedy
- [ ] Systemic issues identified through complaints trigger system review
- [ ] Records of challenges and outcomes are maintained

### ETA Framework Point 5 requirements

- [ ] "Presumption of publication" — algorithms are published with plain-language explanations
- [ ] Warning labels or disclosure when AI is used
- [ ] Accountable officers available for real-time citizen queries
- [ ] Accessible feedback loops for citizens

### Service Standard Point 3: Joined-up experience

- [ ] The challenge process works across all channels (online, phone, in-person, letter)
- [ ] Citizens are not bounced between channels
- [ ] The non-AI channel provides consistent outcomes

### ICO individual rights

- [ ] Right to be informed about AI processing is met
- [ ] Right not to be subject to solely automated decisions with legal/significant effects (Article 22)
- [ ] Right to meaningful human review is provided
- [ ] Right to obtain an explanation of the decision

## Output

1. Contestability process document
2. Citizen-facing guidance on how to challenge decisions
3. Internal process for handling challenges
4. Reporting mechanism for systemic issues
