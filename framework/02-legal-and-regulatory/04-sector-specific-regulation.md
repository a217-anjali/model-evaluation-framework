## Purpose

The UK's AI regulatory approach is context-specific, not blanket. The same LLM carries different regulatory requirements depending on the sector it is deployed in. Existing sector regulators apply the five cross-sectoral principles within their domains.

## Sector Regulators and AI Requirements

| Sector | Regulator | Key AI Considerations |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| Health & Social Care | CQC, MHRA | Patient safety, medical device regulations, clinical validation |
| Financial Services | FCA, PRA | Consumer duty, model risk management, explainability for credit decisions |
| Education | Ofsted, OfS | Safeguarding, accessibility, academic integrity |
| Employment | EHRC | Recruitment bias, automated screening fairness |
| Justice | HMCTS, MoJ | Due process, right to fair trial, judicial independence |
| Policing | HMICFRS, College of Policing | Surveillance, profiling, proportionality |
| Immigration | ICIBI | Automated decision fairness, appeal rights |
| Benefits & Welfare | DWP | Automated entitlement decisions, Article 22 GDPR |
| Transport | DfT, CAA | Safety-critical systems, reliability |
| Communications | Ofcom | Content moderation, misinformation, online safety |

## Requirements

- [ ] Identify which sector regulator(s) apply to your LLM deployment
- [ ] Review sector-specific AI guidance from the relevant regulator
- [ ] Map the five cross-sectoral principles to sector requirements
- [ ] Engage with the regulator early if deploying in a novel context
- [ ] Align LLM deployment with existing organisational policies (AIP Principle 10)

## Output

A regulatory mapping document identifying applicable sector regulations and how compliance will be achieved.
